# City And Production

## Main Principle

The city is not a goal in itself.

The city is a tool.

The player does not think:

> I need a bigger city.

The player thinks:

> I need a bigger forge so I can produce more tools.

## Role Of The City

The city is the player's production, logistics, and trade base.

It should help with:

- processing resources,
- assembling products,
- storing goods,
- optimizing production chains,
- preparing orders,
- selling products.

## Example Buildings

- Warehouses.
- Workshops.
- Sawmills.
- Forges.
- Farms.
- Shops.

## Production

Products should have increasingly complex recipes.

Simple products can be created independently. Advanced products should require
components from other specializations.

Specific production paths are described as production chains. Each chain should
show the path from the first resource to a building, upgrade, or product that
matters to the player economy.

Example:

- the carpenter produces furniture,
- the blacksmith produces screws and steel parts,
- the farmer produces leather, fabric, or food for workers.

## NPC Workers

NPC workers can be part of later progression.

Their role:

- automating simple tasks,
- increasing production scale,
- operating buildings,
- working in factories after many hours of progression.

Workers should not replace player decisions. They should increase the scale of
the operation that the player has already built.
