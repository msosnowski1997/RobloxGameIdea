# Specializations And Skills

## Key Design Problem

If every player can do everything, trade dies.

The game needs to enforce specialization without taking away the player's sense
of freedom.

## Assumption

The player can try different activities, but cannot become a master of
everything.

Specialization should come from two constraints:

- a limited number of talent points,
- the increasing cost of developing higher skill levels.

## Talent Points

Each player has a limited number of talent points.

Example:

- maximum of 100 talent points.

Possible configurations:

- 100 Logging,
- 50 Logging and 50 Smithing,
- 30 Logging, 30 Smithing, and 40 Trading.

It is impossible to max out everything. This forces trade, cooperation, and the
creation of different roles in the server economy.

## Example Skills

- Logging.
- Mining.
- Farming.
- Smithing.
- Carpentry.
- Trading.
- Cooking.

## Specialization Effect

Specialization should give the player a real advantage.

Examples:

- better recipes,
- faster production,
- lower production cost,
- higher product quality,
- access to unique buildings,
- better trade conditions.

## Design Risk

Constraints cannot be too aggressive at the start of the game.

A new player should have time to experiment before deciding on a specialization.
Hard choices should appear only once the player understands the economy and sees
the value of trade.
